Our Story

Born from reverence for India's oldest art traditions and a desire to see them flourish.

Our Origin

From a Village Wall
to Your Wardrobe

In 2020, founder Anshika Bhatia visited a small village in Mithila and watched an elderly woman paint intricate Madhubani motifs on a freshly plastered wall. The woman told Anshika that her granddaughter had no interest in continuing the tradition — there was no livelihood in it anymore.

Anshika returned to Mumbai with samples of hand-painted fabric and a mission: to create a market for folk art clothing that was beautiful enough to be desired, and ethical enough to truly support the artisans who make it. Saudade was born.

Today we work with many artisan families across various states, from the Warli painters of Palghar to the Phulkari embroiderers of Amritsar. Every garment is a collaboration between a master craftsperson's hands and a contemporary designer's eye.

What We Stand For

Our Values

🤝

Fair Trade Always

Every artisan we work with earns at least 3x the local market rate. We publish our payment structures publicly. No exceptions, ever.

🌿

Natural & Sustainable

All dyes are plant or mineral-based. Fabrics are handloom-woven. No synthetic chemicals enter our supply chain.

📚

Preserving Knowledge

We document each art form we work with — their history, techniques, and symbolism — and publish it freely for the world.

👩‍🎨

Women-Led Craft

78% of our artisan partners are women. Folk art is women's art — we centre their skill, their stories, and their leadership.

✦

Authenticity First

No machine prints masquerading as handcraft. Every motif on every garment is hand-applied by a living artisan.

🌍

Global Reach, Local Roots

We ship worldwide while keeping every thread of our supply chain within the communities we serve.
