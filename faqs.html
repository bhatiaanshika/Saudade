Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Saudade, our pieces, and the artisans behind them.

How long does delivery take?

+
Standard delivery across India takes 5–7 working days. Express delivery (2–3 days) is available at checkout for an additional charge. International orders ship in 10–15 working days. Since many of our pieces are handcrafted to order, some items may take slightly longer — we'll always let you know at checkout if that's the case.

Do you offer free shipping?

+
Yes! All orders above ₹2,000 qualify for free standard shipping within India. You can also use the code SHIP0 at checkout for free shipping on your first order, regardless of amount. International shipping rates vary by destination and are calculated at checkout.

Can I track my order?

+
Absolutely. Once your order is dispatched, you'll receive a tracking number via email and SMS. You can use this to track your parcel on our courier partner's website. If you have a Saudade account, you can also view live order status in your account dashboard under "My Orders".

Do you ship internationally?

+
Yes, we ship to over 40 countries worldwide. International shipping typically takes 10–15 business days. Please note that customs duties and import taxes may apply depending on your country, and these are the buyer's responsibility. We declare the true value of goods on all customs forms.

How do I find my right size?

+
Each product page has a detailed size guide with chest, waist, and hip measurements in both inches and centimetres. Our garments run true to size. If you're between sizes, we recommend sizing up for a more relaxed fit — which suits our flowing folk-art styles beautifully. Our customer care team is also happy to help you choose the right size via WhatsApp or email.

Are all pieces truly handmade?

+
Every single piece. We do not use machine prints, digital prints, or screen-printed mass reproductions. Each motif is hand-applied — whether hand-painted (Madhubani, Warli, Kalamkari), hand-embroidered (Phulkari, Chikankari), or hand block-printed (Ajrakh, Bagru). Because of this, minor variations between pieces are normal and make each garment uniquely yours.

What fabrics do you use?

+
We use handloom-woven fabrics exclusively — primarily pure cotton, khadi, and silk, depending on the garment. All fabrics are sourced from weaver cooperatives across India. We never use synthetic fabrics or blended materials that compromise breathability or artisan integrity. Fabric details are listed on each product page.

Can I customise a piece or request a specific size?

+
Yes — we offer customisation on select pieces. This includes custom sizing (for an additional charge), alternative colourways, and in some cases, personalised motifs. Customisation orders typically take 3–4 weeks. Please email us at hello@Saudade.in or WhatsApp us with your requirements and we'll let you know what's possible.

How should I care for my Saudade pieces?

+
Hand wash in cold water with a gentle, pH-neutral detergent. Do not use bleach or fabric softeners. Do not wring — gently press out water and dry flat in shade. Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight, which can fade natural dyes over time. Iron on the reverse side on low heat. Each garment also comes with a printed care card.

What is your return and exchange policy?

+
We offer a 15-day return policy from the date of delivery. Items must be unworn, unwashed, and in their original packaging with tags attached. To initiate a return, email us at hello@Saudade.in with your order number and reason for return. We'll arrange a pickup at no cost to you. Refunds are processed within 5–7 working days of receiving the returned item.

What if my item arrives damaged or incorrect?

+
We're so sorry if that happens. Please photograph the item and contact us within 48 hours of delivery at hello@Saudade.in. We will arrange an immediate replacement or full refund, including return shipping, at absolutely no cost to you. This is our unconditional promise.

How do you ensure fair pay for your artisans?

+
We pay our artisan partners a minimum of 3x the local market rate for comparable work. Artisans receive upfront advances before beginning work, so they are never financially exposed during production. We publish our payment principles on our website and undergo annual fair trade audits. A minimum of 15% of our net profits goes directly into an artisan welfare fund.

Which folk art traditions do you work with?

+
Currently we work with 12 traditions: Madhubani (Bihar), Warli (Maharashtra), Kalamkari (Andhra Pradesh), Phulkari (Punjab), Bandhani (Rajasthan & Gujarat), Ajrakh (Kutch), Pattachitra (Odisha), Chikankari (Uttar Pradesh), Ikat (Telangana & Odisha), Kantha (West Bengal), Gond (Madhya Pradesh), and Pichwai (Rajasthan). We add new traditions as we build trusted partnerships with artisan communities.

Can I visit an artisan community or workshop?

+
Yes! We occasionally organise "Heritage Visits" — curated trips to our artisan communities in Bihar, Maharashtra, Punjab and Rajasthan. These are intimate, small-group experiences where you can watch the craft in action, speak with the artisans, and understand the tradition deeply. Sign up for our newsletter to be notified of upcoming visits.

I am an artisan. How can I partner with Saudade?

+
We are always looking to build new artisan partnerships. Please email us at artisans@Saudade.in with photographs of your work, a brief description of your craft tradition, your location, and the size of your group or collective. Our artisan relations team reviews all applications and responds within two weeks. We particularly welcome applications from underrepresented traditions and women-led collectives.
